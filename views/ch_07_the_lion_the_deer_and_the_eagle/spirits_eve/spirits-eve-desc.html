Winter heralds the coming of bitter cold and dark days. Historically, to prevent evil spirits from creeping too close, frightening decorations and garb alike would adorn people and buildings as they danced. And to guide benevolent spirits, bonfires and candles would light up the night from sunset to sunrise. Traditionally, this gathering is celebrated on the final Sunday of the Wyvern Moon. However, because the Three-Way Battle will take place the following day, Garreg Mach has opted to instead hold it on the second-to-last Sunday. Dress code includes the school uniform (or regular semi-formal clothing for non-students) and a mask of some sort. Leave weapons at the door, silly (well, except for knights) - you're here to mingle and party! Come to the reception hall for a fun night of masquerade, music, and food. Or find a quiet spot and study, maybe, if that's what you prefer.
