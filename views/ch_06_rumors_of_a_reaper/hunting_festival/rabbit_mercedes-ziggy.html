Kingdom of Faerghus - Teutates' Pond
Lying west from Arianrhod, the slightly frigid location in Faerghus is surprisingly serene. The tall and rigid mountains hugging the area keep the air still, as the pond in the middle brings life to it's surroundings. Embellished by small flowers and weeds, fish can be found wriggling about, small game coming from the brush to take a sip of the cold water before it freezes over.

All the birds whistling and the animals jumping around and interacting with each other, it looked surreal... With all the missions and the battles that take place in them, it was nice to go somewhere to a place like this; with the trees rustling because of the soft breeze and the sun's bright light beaming over the animals and plants. Mercedes took a glance at the pond which had the sunbeams reflecting over it. A few animals had taken a few sips out of it and the fish and other water species swam through the water. Though the sun was up and all, the breeze was quite cold. But nonetheless, the scene looked beautiful. So the blonde didn't really understand why her intention here was to hunt... Luckily, she had someone to keep her company and keep her mind off the hunting and such. That someone was Zegrath though, someone who Mercedes didn't really know that well. At least he was from the same house... that was good she guessed. Though talking to someone she didn't really know could be awkward. Maybe she could befriend or learn more about them. The blonde looked around the place to take in the sight of a different, but serene place before hunting or more of asking what to do because Mercedes wasn't really a hunter. "Uhm... Zegrath, was it?" she started, trying to strike up a conversation as to not make hunting awkward, though people probably needed quiet when hunting. The blonde-haired student was unsure though. "The place is nice." She remarked. Was that right to start off a conversation to someone she doesn't know that well? "Shall we start hunting then?" Mercedes gripped her weapon, a dagger, tight and glanced at the animals near the pond once more before looking back up at Zegrath.

Zegrath took in a deep breath of that chilly forest air. Almost nostalgic, really. He'd lived in a forest just like this for a good few years, it was still quite relaxing.
